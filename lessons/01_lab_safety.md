## Rule #1: Dress for Success
Safety in the lab starts before the experiment begins. 

* Long hair must be tied back.
* No dangling jewelry or baggy clothing.
* Closed-toe shoes are mandatory (no sandals).

--

## Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
* **Goggles:** Must be worn over eyes, not on the forehead, whenever heat, glass, or chemicals are used.
* **Aprons:** Protect your clothes and skin from spills.
* **Gloves:** Used when handling corrosive materials.

---

## Turn and Talk
Why do you think "closed-toe shoes" are a strict requirement, even if we aren't working with heavy objects?

--

## The Answer
**Spills and Glass.**
If a beaker breaks or a chemical splashes off the table, gravity pulls it straight to your feet. Fabric sneakers or sandals offer zero protection against liquid chemicals or shards of glass.

---

## Lab Behavior & Workspace
* No food, gum, or drinks are allowed in the lab area.
* Keep aisles clear of backpacks and coats.
* Never "horseplay" or run in the classroom.

## Emergency Procedures: Eye Wash
If a chemical gets in your eyes:
1.  Alert the teacher immediately.
2.  Hold eyelids open.
3.  Flush eyes with water for at least **15-20 minutes**.

---

## Handling Chemicals
* Consider all chemicals dangerous.
* Do not taste or smell chemicals directly.
* Use the **Wafting Technique** to smell vapors.

![Image: Diagram showing a student waving their hand over a test tube toward their nose]

## Red Card / Green Card Check
I will read a scenario. Hold up your **Green Card** if the action is safe, or your **Red Card** if it is dangerous.

--

## Scenario 1
"A student notices a small crack in their test tube but decides to use it anyway because the leak is very slow."

**Answer: RED CARD.** Never use cracked or chipped glassware. Heat can cause cracked glass to shatter instantly.

--

## Scenario 2
"After finishing the lab, a student cleans their station and washes their hands with soap and water before leaving."

**Answer: GREEN CARD.** Always wash your hands, even if you wore gloves!

---

## Cleaning Up
* Dispose of waste in the specific bins designated by the teacher.
* **Never** pour chemicals down the sink unless told to do so.
* Wipe down your lab bench with the provided disinfectant.
